Problem & SolutionAddressing the Challenges

The Problem Statement

There is a critical gap in the current educational landscape that we are addressing:

  • Lack of Appreciation: Arts, culture, and design are undervalued in most Rwandan schools, particularly in Gasabo district.
  • Unexploited Potential: The creativity and innovation potential among children remains largely untapped.
  • Missed Opportunities: This negligence limits young minds from learning creative skills essential for future industries and cultural pride.
  • Educational Context: The Ministry of Education's new reform (June 2025) introduces pathways in Math/Science, Arts/Humanities, and Languages to prepare students for life by 2050.
  • Urgent Need: To maximize this vision, it is urgent to integrate arts, culture, and design throughout the curriculum to foster creativity, innovation, and sustainable development.

Our Solution

Bridging the gap through integrated creative education.

We propose a comprehensive approach to integrate arts, culture, and design into the standard curriculum. By doing so, we aim to:

  • Unlock the creative potential of African children from an early age.
  • Transform overlooked talents into viable opportunities for future careers.
  • Strengthen cultural pride and community identity through education.
  • Foster innovation that drives sustainable development across the continent.
